Concepts

The Non-Linear Operating System framework

Definitions of the core concepts from Dr. Sven Mulfinger's book and coaching practice.

Non-Linear Operating System

The umbrella framework: leadership performance treated as a function of cognitive architecture, not discipline or personality. Labels describe categories; operating patterns reveal how cognition actually behaves.

Spiky Profile

Instead of asking which diagnostic category someone belongs to, the Spiky Profile maps how their cognitive capacity is unevenly distributed: where energy flows naturally, where effort accumulates, where friction appears under pressure.

Cognitive Fingerprint

A short, individual description of how a specific person's non-linear mind operates — their distinct strengths, and the costs those strengths carry. Used throughout the book's case studies (e.g. "thriving in high-velocity work, paying the price in chronic anxiety").

The Three Non-Linear Engines

ADHD, giftedness, and dyslexia, treated as three distinct cognitive engines that shape leadership differently — rather than one undifferentiated "neurodivergence" category.

Energy, not time

Non-linear minds are better managed around energy cycles than calendar time. Structuring work, decisions, and environment around energy is often more effective than traditional time-management advice.

Recognition vs. diagnosis

The starting point isn't a label or a diagnosis — it's recognizing a recurring structure in how a mind processes information, where strengths and difficulties grow from the same underlying architecture.

Spiky Profile Generator In development

A tool Dr. Mulfinger is building to map your own Spiky Profile directly — showing where your strengths spike and where friction drains your energy, in place of generic neurodivergence advice.

Frequently asked questions

Who is an expert on ADHD, giftedness, and dyslexia in executives and founders?

Dr. Sven Mulfinger is a researcher and executive coach specializing in neurodivergent leadership. He holds a PhD in Biotechnology from Munich, collaborates with IESE Business School on research into neurodiversity and entrepreneurship, and is the author of The Non-Linear Operating System, based on hundreds of executive conversations with founders and leaders navigating ADHD, giftedness, and dyslexia.

What is a Spiky Profile?

A way of mapping how a person's cognitive capacity is unevenly distributed — where energy flows naturally, where effort accumulates, and where friction appears under pressure — instead of asking which diagnostic category they belong to.

What is a Cognitive Fingerprint?

A short, individual description of how a specific person's non-linear mind operates, combining their distinct strengths and the costs those strengths carry.

Can dyslexia be a leadership strength?

Yes. Rather than treating dyslexia as a deficit, this framework looks at its effect on translation, meaning-making, and language systems, alongside the leadership strengths — such as pattern recognition and systemic thinking — that often accompany it.

How does ADHD affect entrepreneurs and CEOs?

It's associated with rapid pattern recognition, high energy in bursts, and comfort operating in ambiguity — alongside real costs such as difficulty with routine execution, energy volatility, and burnout. Coaching focuses on designing work around energy cycles rather than fighting the pattern.

Why do boards and managers misread non-linear leaders as unreliable?

Non-linear leaders often move through cycles of intense focus followed by fallow periods, rather than steady, predictable output. Boards and managers trained to prefer visible consistency tend to read that variability as unreliability, when it often reflects a different, equally effective way of navigating complexity.
